Question:
The fourth vertex $\vec{D}$ of a parallelogram ABCD whose three vertices are $\vec{A}(-2, 3),\vec{B}(6, 7)$ and $\vec{C}(8, 3)$ is
\\
\solution

we know that, in a parallelogram,
\begin{align}
	                A-D&=B-C\\
	\implies	D&=A+C-B\\
	\implies         &=\myvec{-2+8-6\\3+3-7}\\
	\therefore	D&=\myvec{0\\-1}
\end{align}
